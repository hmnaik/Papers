Types of Conic Sections

Back to Top

The locus of a point moving on a plane such that its distances from a fixed point and a fixed straight line on the plane are in constant ratio e is called a conic.

The fixed point is called the focus and is usually denoted by S. The fixed straight line is called the directrix. The constant ratio e is called the eccentricity. The straight line on the plane passing through the focus and perpendicular to the directrix is called the axis. Therefore, the locus of a point moving on a plane such that SP/ P M = e , where PM is the perpendicular distance from p to the directrix, is called a conic.

  • if e = 1 the conic is called a parabola
  • if 0 < e < 1 the conic is called an ellipse
  • if e > 1 the conic is called a hyperbola.

Parametric Form of Conic Sections

Back to Top

A conic section is a type of mathematical form in which a curve can be obtained using cones; in simple it is the intersection of a plane and a cone. We have different types of conic sections like Parabola, Circle, Ellipse and Hyperbola.

Parametric Form of Equation

A parametric form is used to define a simple equation using parameters. We can simple represent it using the following example. Consider the parabolic equation y = x2, we can write it into parametric form as shown below

Regular equation of parabola is

y = x2

Parametric equation is

y = t2

Here we replaced ‘x’ with a parameter ‘t’.

We can also use trigonometric parameters such as ‘a cos(t)’ and ‘a sin(t)’ instead of using parameter t, here we can use them as (x, y) = (cos t, sin t) and represented as

x =a cos(t) and

y = a sin(t),

where 0<t<2π.

Example:

x2/25 + y2/16 = 1

Given equation

x2/25 + y2/16 = 1

Instead of 1 we can write it as cos2 t + sin2 t, (since cos2 t + sin2 t = 1)

So now the equation is

x2/25 + y2/16 = cos2 t + sin2 t

We get,

x2/25 = cos2 t

y2/16 = sin2 t

x2 = 25 cos2 t

x = 5 cos t

similarly y2 = 16 sin2 t

y = 4 sin t

The parametric equation is x = 5 cos t, y = 4 sin t

Solving Parametric Equations of Conic Sections

Back to Top

Below are some solved examples on parametric equations of conic sections

Example 1: Find the parametric equation for the equation x2 + y2 + 12x + 4y - 3 = 0

Solution:

Given equation

x2 + y2 + 12x + 4y - 3 = 0

now take

x = cos t and y = sin t and (cos2 t + sin2 t = 1)

cos2 t + sin2 t + 12 cos t + 4 sin t - 3 = 0

( cos2 t + sin2 t) + 12 cos t + 4 sin t - 3 = 0

1 + 12 cos t + 4 sin t - 3 = 0

12 cos t + 4 sin t - 2 = 0

12 cos t + 4 sin t = 2

12/2 cos t + 4/2 sin t = 1

6 cos t + 2 sin t = 1

The parametric equation is 6 cos t + 2 sin t = 1.

Example 2: Find the parametric equation for the following equation x2/4 - y2/36 = 1

solution:

Given equation

x2/4 - y2/36 = 1

Instead of 1 we can write it as cos2 t + sin2 t, (since cos2 t + sin2 t = 1)

So now the equation is

x2/4 - y2/36 = cos2 t + sin2 t

We get,

x2/4 = cos2 t

y2/36 = sin2 t

x2 = 4 cos2 t

x = 2 cos t

similarly y2 = 36 sin2 t

y = 6 sin t

The parametric equation is x = 2 cos t, y = 6 sin t
